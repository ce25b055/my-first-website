Great Indian Personalities and Their Way of Life

Visvesvaraya

M Visvesvaraya, Early Life, Contributions, Awards, DeathThe "Father of Indian Engineering" is widely considered to be Sir Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya, a legendary civil engineer, statesman, and Bharat Ratna recipient, known for his visionary contributions to nation-building, flood protection systems in Hyderabad, and iconic projects like the Krishna Raja Sagara Dam, with his birthday celebrated as India's Engineers' Day.

Tenali Ramakrishna

Tenali Rama, a witty Telugu poet in King Krishnadevaraya's court, often used humor and intelligence to solve problems, teaching lessons to arrogant courtiers and the king; one famous tale involves him tricking ministers by "disappearing" canals on paper, proving his cleverness over bureaucratic records, while another shows his wit when his cat refuses milk at a royal contest, winning the prize for being unique.

Swami Vivekananda

Vivekananda was once extremely poor, often going hungry, yet he never complained, even telling his mother he'd eaten when he hadn't. His guru, Ramakrishna, knowing this, sent him to the temple of Goddess Kali to ask for food and worldly comforts. Narendra went in, but instead of asking for anything, he simply sat, overwhelmed with gratitude for his life, body, and senses, and came out without a word.
